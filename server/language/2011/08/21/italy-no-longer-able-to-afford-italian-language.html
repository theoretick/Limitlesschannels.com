Italy no longer able to afford Italian Language

21 Aug 2011 | language | author: theoretick

Apparently the foremost institute for maintenance of the Italian language (think 'L'academie francaise' of Italy) can no longer sustainably afford to fund its own language.  The struggle between people who try and govern the language with an iron fist and economics has always been an interesting and arbitrary one.  Still, it is one that a shockingly large subset of people think is necessary and there are many languages around the world with such similar institutions (see Wikipedia's List of Language Regulators).
