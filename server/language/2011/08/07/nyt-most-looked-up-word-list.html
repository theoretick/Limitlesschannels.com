NYT Most Looked-up word list

07 Aug 2011 | language | author: theoretick

NewYorkTimes.com has a built-in dictionary where readers can instantly look up any word that is used in an article.  NYT keeps track of this data and has recently published this year's "Most frequently looked-up words" list.

via NYTimes

 

Meanwhile, I've been reading Ulysses and picked up half a dozen more, have at thee:

consubstantial - Of the same substance or essence (used esp. of the three persons of the Trinity in Christian theology)

coign - A projecting corner or angle of a wall or building.

moiety - Each of two parts into which a thing is or can be divided.

postprandial - During or relating to the period after dinner or lunch.

jakes - toilet, esp. an outdoor one

offal - The entrails and internal organs of an animal used as food.

cad - A man who behaves dishonorably, esp. toward a woman.

 

Just like everything else I try to learn I've compiled these into a convenient deck to be used in Anki, which you can grab HERE.  I would however recommend that anyone with the time actually enter these manually since the process of inputting them actually does contribute to memorization.
